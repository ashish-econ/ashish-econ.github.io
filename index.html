Hi, welcome to my webpage!

My name is Christine Cai and I'm currently a first-year Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at Princeton University.

My research interests (which might be subject to changes...!) currently lie in applied microeconomics (this field of interest is unlikely to change though!) and include education/labor, development, and urban economics. I am notably interested in issues related to educational inequalities, social mobility, and segregation.

I previously worked as a full-time research assistant for Professor Rebecca Dizon-Ross at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business (2016-2018) and for Professor Ruben Durante at the Department of Economics of Sciences Po Paris (2015-2016).

I hold a Master's degree in Economics from Sciences Po Paris. I wrote my Master's thesis – entitled 'Colonial Administrators and Public Educational Investments in French West Africa' – under the supervision of Professor Quoc-Anh Do, and successfully defended it in July 2015 [paper] [slides].
